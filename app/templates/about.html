Yapi Kapi translates to remember your story in the language of the Lakota Tribe of North Dakota.

Yapi Kapi is a tool dedicated to discovering the history and culture of Native American tribes in California using the vast collection of the Phoebe A. Hearst Museum of Anthropology. Through the interactive map, students can discover artifacts that belong to different tribes throughout the area and build a personal collection of favorite items in their Satchel.

For students: Students can log in as individuals or as part of a classroom account run by their teachers. Then they can build a collection of favorite artifacts in their personal Satchel, discovering specimens from different cultures or areas of California. Within the Satchel, students can compare and contrast their collection items and take notes about what they've learned about each artifact.

For teachers: Teachers can create a classroom account with individual sub-accounts for each student, relay assignments to the sub-accounts, and view and comment on students' artifact collections. Yapi Kapi is designed as a classroom tool, aligning with the Common Core State Standards Initiative, to facilitate learning through hands-on exploration.
